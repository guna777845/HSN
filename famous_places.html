UNESCO Heritage
01

Belur Chennakeshava Temple

A masterpiece of Hoysala craftsmanship, its outer walls densely covered with over 650 intricately carved friezes — deities, dancers, elephants, and celestial beings frozen in eternal stone.

UNESCO Heritage
02

Halebidu Hoysaleswara Temple

The ancient capital of the Hoysala Empire, home to twin temples whose friezes narrate Hindu epics in extraordinary sculptural relief — a pilgrimage for art lovers and historians alike.

Explore
03

Shettihalli Church Ruins

A submerged Gothic relic that rises from the Hemavathi reservoir each monsoon season, its haunting arches reflected in still water — one of Karnataka's most atmospheric sights.

Explore
04

Hemavathi Reservoir

Serene waters flanked by the Western Ghats' green foothills — a sanctuary for migratory birds and a beloved escape where the horizon softens into emerald hills at dusk.

Explore
05

Manjarabad Fort

Tipu Sultan's star-shaped star fort crowns a 920-metre hill, its geometric ramparts offering panoramic views across a rippling ocean of forested valleys and coffee estates.

Explore
06

Shravanabelagola Gommateshvara

Atop Vindhyagiri Hill stands the world's tallest monolithic statue — the 18-metre Bahubali, carved from a single granite outcrop in 981 CE, commanding silence across the plains.
